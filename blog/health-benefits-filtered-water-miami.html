Health Benefits of Filtered Water for Miami Families

CrystalFlow Miami Team|March 5, 2025|5 min read

Miami tap water meets EPA legal standards, but meeting legal standards does not mean optimal health. The Environmental Working Group's Tap Water Database reports that Miami-Dade County water contains 12 contaminants above health-based guidelines — levels that exceed what independent scientists recommend for long-term safety, even if they remain within legal limits.

What Miami Tap Water Contains

Miami-Dade water regularly contains:

  • Total trihalomethanes (TTHMs): Disinfection byproducts formed when chloramine reacts with organic matter. Associated with increased cancer risk at chronic exposure levels.
  • Haloacetic acids (HAAs): Another class of disinfection byproducts with similar health concerns.
  • Chromium-6: A carcinogen detected above EWG health guidelines in Miami water.
  • PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances): Detected in trace amounts and linked to immune system disruption and cancer risk by the EPA.
  • Chloramine: Used as a disinfectant, it causes the characteristic smell and taste of Miami tap water and produces TTHMs as a byproduct.

Health Benefits of Filtered Water for Your Family

Reduced Exposure to Disinfection Byproducts

Reverse osmosis systems remove 99%+ of TTHMs, HAAs, and chloramine from drinking water. For families consuming multiple liters of water daily, this is a meaningful reduction in chronic exposure to compounds associated with long-term health risk.

PFAS Removal

NSF/ANSI 58-certified reverse osmosis membranes remove PFAS compounds effectively. Given that PFAS are persistent chemicals that accumulate in the body over time, reducing exposure through drinking water is one of the most practical steps Miami families can take.

Lead Removal

While Miami's municipal water supply does not contain elevated lead levels at the treatment plant, older home plumbing can leach lead into the water between the meter and the tap. Reverse osmosis removes 99%+ of lead, protecting children and pregnant women who are most vulnerable to its effects.

Better Hydration

The most underrated benefit of filtered water is simple: it tastes better. Miami tap water carries a distinctive chloramine odor that many residents describe as off-putting. Filtered water has no smell and a clean, neutral taste — making it easier for adults and children to drink the recommended daily amount.

Which System Provides the Best Health Protection?

For Miami families, a reverse osmosis system provides the most comprehensive health protection at the tap. RO removes 99%+ of the contaminants that appear above health guidelines in Miami water.
