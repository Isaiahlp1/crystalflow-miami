How Miami Hard Water Is Damaging Your Appliances (And How to Stop It)

CrystalFlow Miami Team|February 26, 2025|5 min read

If you own a home in Miami-Dade County, hard water is quietly costing you money every single day. The mineral-heavy water flowing through your pipes deposits layers of calcium and magnesium scale inside your water heater, dishwasher, washing machine, and plumbing.

What Is Water Hardness and Why Does Miami Have It?

Miami draws the majority of its drinking water from the Biscayne Aquifer, a shallow groundwater source beneath thick layers of porous limestone. Water typically measures between 15 and 25 grains per gallon (GPG) in hardness — more than double the threshold for "hard" water per the Water Quality Association.

How Hard Water Destroys Your Water Heater

Your water heater is the appliance most vulnerable to hard water damage. Scale buildup can cause a 15% reduction in heating efficiency, according to the U.S. Department of Energy. In Miami, water heaters commonly fail within 6 to 8 years — half the expected lifespan with soft water.
